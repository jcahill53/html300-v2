Some Fun Facts About the Family Crest

The name: The most common Irish surname, Murphy is believed to be derived from the old Irish surname Ó Murchadha meaning ‘Son of the Sea Warrior’.

The crest: The red of the Murphy crest represents martyrdom while the black to the crest’s centre symbolises fidelity. The wheat is representative of plentifulness – fitting given that the surname is Ireland’s most common.

The motto: “Fortis es hospitalis”, meaning “Brave and hospitable”.

Source: The Irish Post March 01, 2017
